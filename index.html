One Small Task

Today's Cleaning

Wipe light switches and door handles throughout the house

Easy to overlook, surprisingly grimy. Grab a damp microfiber with a touch of all-purpose cleaner (or a disinfecting wipe) and walk through every room hitting switches, doorknobs, cabinet pulls, and the fridge handle. Takes less time than you expect; leaves the whole house feeling subtly fresher.

Time: 15–25 minutes. Bonus (Spring): rinse one window screen while you're already up.

Today's Financial Term

Earnings Per Share (EPS)

What it is — EPS is a company's profit divided by the number of outstanding shares. If a company earns $1 billion and has 500 million shares out, EPS = $2. It's a quick way to see how much profit "belongs" to each share you own.

Quick example — When Apple reports a quarter, headlines like "Apple beats: EPS $2.40 vs. $2.30 expected" mean Apple made 10 cents more per share than Wall Street predicted — and that gap usually moves the stock.

Why it matters — EPS is the building block of valuation. Combined with the share price, it gives you the P/E ratio (price-to-earnings), the most-quoted measure of whether a stock is "expensive" or "cheap." If you only learn one company number, learn EPS.
